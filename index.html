Level 1: What is shutter speed?


In short, shutter speed is the amount of time that the shutter is open. (shutter speed = exposure time)


slower shutter speed -> the image sensor get more light -> the photo looks brighter!


Must Knows about Shutter Speed

  • Shutter speed is measured in seconds or in most cases fractions of seconds. The bigger the denominator the faster the speed (ie 1/1000 is much faster than 1/30).
  • In most cases you will probably be using shutter speeds of 1/60th of a second or faster. This is because anything slower than this is very difficult to use without getting camera shake. Camera shake is when your camera is moving while the shutter is open and results in blur in your photos.
  • If you are using a slow shutter speed (anything slower than 1/60) you will need to either use a tripod or some some type of image stabilization (more and more cameras are coming with this built in).

Level 2: What is Shutter Speed Priority Mode?

Shutter Priority Mode simply means that you set the shutter speed and the camera chooses the aperture.

Second

 rotate the main dial button to adjust the shutter speed



Third

 on the LCD monitor, you can see the change of shutter speed and you can ajust the shutter speed to the ideal number you want
